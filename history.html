Hubble History

Purpose

The purpose of the Hubble Space Telescope was to obtain images & explore the universe using infrared, visible light, and ultraviolet wavelengths. These images have helped understand space by visulizing and collecting data on cosmic objects.

Launch Date

The Hubble Space Telescope was launched by NASA in 1990, although it was first conceived in the 1940s.

Mission End Date

  • Hubble has no mission end date, and will continue to operate until failure.
  • A misconception many people have today is that the Hubble Space Telescope is no longer in use. In reality, the Hubble is still in use today, and is still observing cosmic objects, along with the James Webb Telescope.

Who Worked on the Hubble?

The Hubble space telescope was worked on by multiple people and organizations.

  • The development of Hubble was first started by NASA in the 1970s, inspired by Lyman Spitzer, who was a Princeton astrophysicist. His paper, named "Astronomical advantages of an extraterrestrial observatory," played a pivotal role in laying the foundation of the Hubble project.
  • Shortly before development was started, the European Space Agency joined the project and contributed 15 percent of the funding for the Hubble. Because of this, NASA allowed ESA at least 15 percent of telescope time.
  • Lockheed Missiles and Space Company, now called Lockheed Martin, built the spacecraft in which the Hubble was housed.
  • Perkin-Elmer Corporation built the Optical Telescope Assembly and the Fine Guidance Sensors.

How Has the Hubble Aided in Space Exploration?

The Hubble has aided greatly in space exploration, possibly more than any other instrument created by mankind. For some perspective, when the Hubble was launched in 1990, no planets around other stars had been discovered. Since then, Hubble has taken images of the universe by working in UV, infrared, and visible light, allowing scientists to make significant advancements in astronomy.
