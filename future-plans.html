Hubble Future Plans

Is the Hubble Still In Use?

Yes, the Hubble is still in use, and will continue to operate until failure.

Future of Hubble?

  • In the past, Hubble had regular servicing missions using the Space Shuttle fleet. However, the Space Shuttle was retired, so the Hubble will no longer receive servicing missions.
  • While Hubble is still operational, it will continue to carry out its mission and complement the James Webb Space Telescope by conducting observations in the visible and ultraviolet spectrums.
  • Once Hubble's mission ends, which will likely to be due to its gyros, NASA will prepare Hubble for a controlled reentry into the Earth's atmosphere.

How Has the Hubble Aided in Space Exploration?

The Hubble has aided greatly in space exploration, possibly more than any other instrument created by mankind. For some perspective, when the Hubble was launched in 1990, no planets around other stars had been discovered.

  • By observing distant galaxies and measuring redshift, Hubble has been able to provide evidence that the universe is expanding.
  • Through observing distant supernovas, Hubble has provided evidence that dark energy, a force that causes galaxies to move away from each other, exists.
  • By determining the tangential velocity of the Andromeda Galaxy using the Hubble, scientists were able to predict the future collision between the Milky Way Galaxy and the Andromeda Galaxy.
  • Scientists used the Hubble to observe pulsating stars known as Cephid variables, allowing them to estimate the age of the universe within 5 percent. The current estimate is around 13.82 billion years.
